## 1. Rights of foreign domestic helpers

**Question:** What are the rights of foreign domestic helpers in Hong Kong?

**Confidence:** 0.525

**Answer:**

As a foreign domestic helper in Hong Kong, you have several important rights concerning your personal documents and employment conditions:

1.  **Right to keep your own personal identification documents:** You should keep your own personal identification documents (e.g., identity card, passport). No other person, including your employer or staff of the employment agency, should keep these documents for you without your explicit consent. Withholding your personal property without explicit consent may constitute an offence. (Source 1, page 19; Source 5, page 6)
2.  **Consent for temporary keeping of documents:** If an employment agency needs to temporarily keep your passport or personal identification documents (e.g., when arranging for you to work in Hong Kong), they must explain the reason to you and seek your *written consent*. They must also provide you with a *written acknowledgement* upon obtaining the document. (Source 2, page 30; Source 4, page 124)
3.  **Return of documents:** After obtaining your employment or relevant visa, the employment agency should return your passport or personal identification document directly to you without delay and record the return in writing. (Source 2, page 30)
4.  **Prohibition against withholding documents for payment:** Employment agencies should not withhold your passport, personal identification documents, or employment contracts to force you to pay or repay any sum of money, such as training fees, fees for arranging your arrival in Hong Kong, or loans. (Source 2, page 30)
5.  **Right to withdraw consent:** You may withdraw your consent for the temporary keeping of your documents at any time. (Source 4, page 125)
6.  **Right to be paid correctly:** Employers are legally obligated to pay the Minimum Allowable Wage (MAW) stated on your Standard Employment Contract (SEC). It is an offence for any person, including employment agencies, to aid or abet an employer in making a false statement about paying MAW on the SEC but subsequently underpaying you during employment. (Source 1, page 19)

Please remember that this information is for support purposes only and does not constitute legal advice.

## 2. Recruitment agency rules

**Question:** What are the rules for recruitment agencies?

**Confidence:** 0.525

**Answer:**

Recruitment agencies (EAs) have specific rules regarding job seekers' personal documents:

1.  **Withholding Documents:** EAs must not withhold the personal property of job seekers, including passports, personal identification documents, employment contracts, bank cards, or school certificates, without their explicit consent. Doing so may constitute an offence, such as under the Theft Ordinance (Cap. 210), which carries a maximum penalty of imprisonment for 10 years upon conviction on indictment [Source 1, page 19]. EAs should not withhold these documents to force job seekers to pay or repay any sum of money, such as training fees or loans, regardless of when the sum arose [Source 2, page 30].

2.  **Temporary Keeping of Documents:**
    *   If an EA needs to obtain and temporarily keep a job seeker's passport or personal identification documents (e.g., when arranging non-local job seekers to work in Hong Kong), the EA must explain the reason(s) to the job seeker and seek their **written consent** [Source 2, page 30].
    *   Upon obtaining the document, the EA must provide the job seeker with a **written acknowledgment** [Source 2, page 30].
    *   Once the employment or relevant visa is obtained, the EA should return the passport or personal identification document directly to the job seeker without delay and record the return in writing [Source 2, page 30].
